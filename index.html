[翻译]Knitting.com 第1天 - 选择一个利基市场并为一个域名支付8万美元

Knitting.com Day 1 – Choosing a Niche and Paying $80,000 for a Domain

Knitting.com第1天 - 择一个利基市场并为一个域名支付8万美元

Mike and I have just started a brand new brand (our first time working directly together on an brand) , knitting.com.

迈克和我刚刚开始了一个全新的品牌（我们第一次直接在一个品牌上合作） , knitting.com。

This will mark the first of a series of blog posts and podcasts that we’ll be doing over the next 12 months, chronicling the building of knitting.com into what we aim to be a 7-figure brand within 12 months and an 8-figure brand within 5 years. This will be a similar series to what was done a few years ago as I had a series on building Offroading Gear from scratch.

这将标志着我们将在未来12个月内进行的一系列博文和播客的第一篇，记录了将knitting.com建设成我们的目标是在12个月内成为一个7位数的品牌，在5年内成为一个8位数的品牌。这将是一个类似于几年前的系列，因为我有一个关于从头开始建立Offroading Gear的系列。

In this blog post, I’ll detail how we chose the niche we’ll be getting into as well as the negotiation and valuation that went into purchasing the domain.

在这篇博文中，我将详细介绍我们如何选择我们将要进入的利基市场，以及购买域名的谈判和估价过程。

For the rest of 2022 we’ll be using Knitting.com as a real life case-study for our Secret Sauce membership. Each month we’ll detail one particular thing we’re working on right now for knitting.com from product development to the content templates we use for all of our brands, that you can use to start and grow your own business. Our next class is on February 23. It’s all about niche selection and competitor analysis. Get the Secret Sauce for your brand.

在2022年剩下的时间里，我们将使用Knitting.com作为我们的Secret Sauce会员的真实案例研究。每个月我们将详细介绍我们现在为knitting.com所做的一件特别的事情，从产品开发到我们用于所有品牌的内容模板，你可以用来启动和发展自己的业务。我们的下一堂课是在2月23日。这是关于利基选择和竞争者分析。为你的品牌获取秘诀。

Goals and Business Plan for Knitting.com

编织网的目标和商业计划

Knitting.com will be an ecommerce brand supported heavily by a content website. This follows in a similar path to other projects we’ve had with Offroading.com and Tactical.com. We’re big believers that having a strong _off_-Amazon presence is critical to building an _on_-Amazon brand.

Knitting.com将是一个得到内容网站大力支持的电子商务品牌。这与我们在Offroading.com和Tactical.com的其他项目有类似的路径。我们坚信，拥有一个强大的Off/Amazon存在对于建立一个on/Amazon品牌至关重要。

In a future blog post, I’ll go into complete detail of our entire Traction EOS plan, including our full vision and goals. However, on a very high level our ultimate goal is to be the number one knitting brand on Amazon and our revenue goals are as follows:

I

  • Get to $80,000/month (a million dollar run-rate) within 12 months (February 2023)

  • 在12个月内（2023年2月）达到80,000美元/月（百万美元的运行速率）。

  • Get to $250,000/month ($3million run-rate) within 36 months (February 2025)

  • 在36个月内（2025年2月）达到25万美元/月（300万美元的运行速率）。

  • Get to $625,000/month ($7.5million run-rate) within 50 months (February 2027)

  • 在50个月内（2027年2月）达到62.5万美元/月（750万美元的运行速率）。

Build vs. Buy: Deciding to Build a Business from Scratch Rather than Buying

建设与购买。决定从头开始建立一个企业，而不是购买

Our first decision when deciding to develop a new brand involved the classic dilemma: build vs. buy. Do we buy an existing website/brand with real revenue or build something from scratch? We had a budget of $250,000 to $500,000 we wanted to spend on this brand/project to start, and our initial intention was to buy an existing website. There are pros and cons to buying an existing website with real revenue:

在决定开发一个新品牌时，我们的第一个决定涉及经典的两难问题：建设与购买。我们是购买一个有实际收入的现有网站/品牌，还是从头开始建立一些东西？我们有一个25万到50万美元的预算，我们想在这个品牌/项目上开始花费，我们最初的意图是购买一个现有的网站。购买一个有实际收入的现有网站有其优点和缺点。

Buying a Website: Pros

购买一个网站：优点

  • Existing and predictable revenue

  • 现有的和可预测的收入

  • Less time-intensive to get up and running

  • 启动和运行所需的时间较少

  • Easy(ish) to get lending to buy an existing business

  • 购买现有企业容易获得贷款

Buying a Website: Cons

购买一个网站：弊端

  • Must pay 4x-5x EBITDA (or more)

  • 必须支付4-5倍的EBITDA（或更多）。

  • Highly competitive seller’s market where you’re competing with heavily funded aggregators

  • 竞争激烈的卖方市场，你要与资金雄厚的聚合商竞争

  • Limited supply

  • 供应有限

  • Time-intensive buying process

  • 时间密集的购买过程

Mike and I searched for a business to buy for well over a year and we came up with NOTHING. The biggest issue was that most businesses available for under $500,000 were pretty low quality. For example, there were a lot of businesses with single hero SKUs that were highly reliant on ranking well for a couple of keywords. Other businesses were one-man shops being run so efficiently by their owners it left little room for improvement. If we’re paying a 2.5x multiple for a website like the good ol’ days, these issues wouldn’t have been as significant. But when you’re often paying 4.5x or more for a business, it creates a lot of risks.

迈克和我为购买企业寻找了一年多的时间，我们一无所获。最大的问题是，大多数低于50万美元的企业质量很差。例如，有很多企业都有单一的英雄SKU，高度依赖几个关键词的排名。还有一些企业是单人店，由其业主高效经营，几乎没有改进的余地。如果我们像过去那样为一个网站支付2.5倍的费用，这些问题就不会那么严重。但是，当你经常为一个企业支付4.5倍或更多时，就会产生很多风险。

So after killing a year searching for the perfect business to buy, we realized that in the amount of time we were spending looking for a business, we could build one from scratch more quickly. Again, this had largely to do with the fact that we were looking to buy a business with <$1 million in revenue. A $1 million revenue business is relatively “easy” to do from scratch (see my Offroading Rebuilding a $1million brand series). A $5 million revenue business is a lot harder.

因此，在花了一年时间寻找完美的企业后，我们意识到，在我们寻找企业的时间里，我们可以更快地从头开始建立一个企业。同样，这在很大程度上与我们正在寻找购买一个收入低于100万美元的企业这一事实有关。一个100万美元收入的企业从零开始是相对 “容易 “的（见我的越野车重建100万美元品牌系列）。一个500万美元收入的企业则难得多。

Picking a Niche to Build a Brand In

挑选一个利基市场来建立一个品牌

After deciding to build something from scratch, we had to pick a niche to build the business in. We had a few criteria for the niche we were getting into:

在决定从头开始建立一些东西之后，我们必须选择一个利基市场来建立这个业务。我们对我们要进入的利基市场有几个标准。

  • One with a reasonably sized audience of passionate customers well-defined in a Facebook audience

  • 一个拥有合理规模的热情客户的受众，在Facebook的受众中被很好地定义了。

  • Opportunity for a wide product catalog

  • 有机会获得广泛的产品目录

  • Opportunity to improve on existing products

  • 改善现有产品的机会

  • Opportunity for lots of content, ideally with potential for a digital product

  • 有机会提供大量的内容，最好是有潜力的数字产品

  • Not saturated on Amazon

  • 在亚马逊上没有饱和

On a drive from Las Vegas to Palm Springs, we debated about many niches. Mike and I both often default to hobbies for choosing the niches we get into. Model railroading was one of the potential niches we had both come up with.

在从拉斯维加斯到棕榈泉的车程中，我们对许多壁龛进行了辩论。迈克和我都经常以爱好来选择我们进入的利基市场。铁路模型是我们两个人想出的潜在利基之一。

One of the things we do in our research phase is to search for the main keyword in AHREFS and look at the number of estimated searches and keyword difficulty. Unfortunately, it turned out that for model railroading, the number of searches was low (we like to see searches for primary keywords in the thousands or low ten-thousands and not hundreds), and it’s super hard to rank for those keywords.

我们在研究阶段做的一件事是在AHREFS中搜索主要关键词，看看估计的搜索数量和关键词难度。 不幸的是，结果发现对于铁路模型来说，搜索量很低（我们喜欢看到主要关键词的搜索量在数千或低万，而不是数百），而且这些关键词的排名超级难。

We debated developing a model railroading brand. However, the audience size and competitiveness turned out to be too much.

我们辩论过开发一个铁路模型品牌。然而，观众的规模和竞争力原来是太多了。

Fishing was another one of the niches that made the shortlist. Contrary to model railroading, the audience is huge. However, the competition is also extremely fierce, and, more importantly, we thought we would have a very difficult time improving on any existing products: fishing lures and fishing poles are fairly basic products that are tough to improve on.

钓鱼是另一个进入候选名单的利基市场。与铁路模型不同的是，这个行业的受众非常庞大。 然而，竞争也是非常激烈的，更重要的是，我们认为我们将很难在任何现有产品的基础上进行改进：鱼饵和鱼竿是相当基本的产品，很难改进。

After much deliberation, we came back to a niche Mike had considered many years ago: knitting. It checked most of our boxes: the audience is big enough but not too big (knitting has an AHREFS keyword search volume of 41k), the amount of content opportunities is massive (the number of patterns you can offer is endless), and there’s a decent number of products you can offer in the niche. The biggest challenge is that two middle-aged straight men don’t exactly fit the typical knitter profile, which I’ll address shortly.

经过深思熟虑，我们回到了迈克多年前考虑过的一个利基市场：针织。它满足了我们的大部分要求：受众足够大，但不是太大（针织的AHREFS关键词搜索量为4.1万），内容机会的数量是巨大的（你可以提供的图案数量是无穷无尽的），而且在这个利基中你可以提供相当数量的产品。最大的挑战是，两个中年直男并不完全符合典型的编织者特征，我很快就会解决这个问题。

Spending $80,000 on a Domain (and Is It Worth It?)

花费80,000美元购买域名（值得吗？）

Mike and I both are strong believers in the importance of a strong domain name for any of our brands. We currently own tactical.com and offroading.com, and we’ve had premium top-level domain names for other businesses as well. There’s a certain vanity in it (domains are basically the first NFTs!), but it’s also practical. A premium domain gives you authority and trust not just with your customers and readers, but also with people you’ll work with such as vendors and influencers. Especially as a new brand, having that credibility out of the gate is important.

迈克和我都坚信一个强大的域名对我们任何品牌的重要性。我们目前拥有 tactical.com 和 offroading.com，而且我们也为其他业务拥有高级顶级域名。这有一定的虚荣心（域名基本上是第一个NFT！），但它也很实用。一个高级域名不仅给你的客户和读者带来权威和信任，而且也给你的合作者，如供应商和影响者带来权威和信任。特别是作为一个新的品牌，从一开始就拥有这种信誉是很重要的。

By some weird happenstance, Mike had been in discussions with the owner of the domain name several years prior. The man was an older guy in his sixties who had purchased the domain name over 10 years ago. When Mike had first talked to him, he had wanted $150,000 for the domain. We both agreed that this was well above what we wanted to pay, but also that the name was probably worth close to 6 figures.

由于一些奇怪的巧合，迈克在几年前曾与该域名的所有者进行过讨论。这个人是一个60多岁的老人，10多年前就购买了这个域名。当迈克第一次与他交谈时，他想用15万美元购买这个域名。我们都认为这远远超过了我们想要支付的价格，但也认为这个名字可能价值接近6位数。

麦克和戴夫的针织品

One of the things we did prior to committing all-in to the idea of knitting.com was to do some competitive research at local craft stores to see the depth of the market.

在全身心投入编织网的想法之前，我们做的一件事是在当地的工艺品商店做一些竞争性研究，看看市场的深度。

Domain buying is one of the most unique buying transactions you can ever have. There’s no formula to value a domain, and the uniqueness of a domain name means there’s never a direct comparison. It’s also a highly illiquid market where a domain owner will often sit on a domain name for years—meaning timing is everything. Catching a motivated seller, therefore, is important.

域名购买是你可能遇到的最独特的购买交易之一。没有任何公式来评估域名的价值，而且域名的独特性意味着永远没有直接的比较。这也是一个流动性很差的市场，域名所有者往往会在一个域名上坐上好几年–这意味着时机就是一切。因此，抓住一个积极的卖家是很重要的。

Normally, a seller’s asking price for a domain name is significantly above their real price. My experience is that it’s roughly 2-3x f what they actually want to get. After a bunch of back and forth, we ultimately agreed with the seller on $80,000 for the domain. So $80,000 and $500 in escrow fees later, the domain was ours.

通常情况下，卖家对域名的要价会大大高于他们的实际价格。我的经验是，这大约是他们实际想得到的价格的2-3倍。在来回折腾之后，我们最终与卖家达成协议，以8万美元购买该域名。因此，8万美元和500美元的托管费之后，这个域名就是我们的了。

Is it necessary to have a premium name like this to build a brand? Definitely not. There’s enough DollarShave Club.com and other massive brands built on fanciful names to say otherwise. But as mentioned, it’s going to give us credibility right out of the gate (and this credibility has already started to pay dividends, as I’ll talk about in the next update). There’ll also be a pretty significant SEO boost from having the domain. Google still gives a very strong preference for keyword domains.

是否有必要拥有这样的高级名称来建立一个品牌？绝对不是。有足够多的DollarShave Club.com和其他建立在梦幻般的名字上的巨大品牌可以说明这一点。 但如前所述，它将使我们在一开始就具有可信度（而且这种可信度已经开始产生红利，我将在下一次更新中谈到）。有了这个域名，也会有一个相当大的SEO提升。谷歌仍然对关键词域名给予非常大的偏爱。

The most important argument for spending a bunch of money in a domain is that it now has us significantly invested in the project (How many $8 Godaddy domains do you have that you never did anything with? I know I have a lot!) It’s a lot harder to forget about the $80,000 domain name you paid for.

在域名上花一大笔钱的最重要的论据是，它现在让我们在项目上有了很大的投资（你有多少个8美元的Godaddy域名，你从来没有做过什么？我知道我有很多！) 要忘记你花钱买的8万美元的域名，那就难上加难了。

Building a Content Website and a Product Brand on the Same Website

在同一个网站上建立一个内容网站和一个产品品牌

There’s a weird paradox with domain names and brands—a perfect domain for a content website like knitting.com is often a terrible name for a product brand. For a bunch of reasons, not the least of which is Amazon, our brand name cannot be knitting.com.

域名和品牌之间有一个奇怪的悖论–对于像knitting.com这样的内容网站来说，一个完美的域名对于一个产品品牌来说往往是一个糟糕的名字。由于一系列的原因，其中最重要的是亚马逊，我们的品牌名称不能是knitting.com。

For our brand name, we’ve decided on a “clever” variation of the word knitting.com, which will have a strong relationship to our domain name but also be unique enough to not get into trouble with Amazon or the trademark office. Stay tuned for future updates on what this brand name actually is.

对于我们的品牌名称，我们已经决定采用knitting.com这个词的 “聪明 “变体，这将与我们的域名有很强的关系，但也足够独特，不会在亚马逊或商标局那里惹上麻烦。请继续关注未来关于这个品牌名称的更新。

There’s also the question of whether you host your e-commerce store and content site on different domains or on the same domain name. There are arguments for both sides. Different domains keep everything separate and cleaner but take roughly twice as much work. In this case though, we’ve decided that due to the value of the knitting.com domain, our content website will be knitting.com, and our shop will be something along the lines of shop.knitting.com.

还有一个问题是，你是把你的电子商务商店和内容网站放在不同的域名上，还是放在同一个域名上。双方都有争论。不同的域名使所有的东西都分开，而且更干净，但需要大约两倍的工作量。在这种情况下，我们已经决定，由于knitting.com域名的价值，我们的内容网站将是knitting.com，而我们的商店将是类似shop.knitting.com的东西。

There’s no right answer for everyone on the brand name or domain name question when developing an e-commerce brand supported by a content brand, but we think the approach we’re taking in this case is the best answer for us.

在发展由内容品牌支持的电子商务品牌时，关于品牌名称或域名问题，没有每个人的正确答案，但我们认为我们在这种情况下采取的方法对我们来说是最佳答案。

Final Thoughts

最后的思考

The purchase of knitting.com was definitely the biggest domain name purchase I’ve ever had. (Mike, on the other hand, has a couple of domain purchases that exceeded this.) I personally have very little concern or doubt about spending this much money on a domain because it’s nearly entirely risk-free: the domain name will always have some intrinsic value in it. As I’ll write about in the next part of this series though, we’ve taken on some expenditures recently which are nearly as large and not quite as risk-free.

购买knitting.com绝对是我有史以来最大的一次域名购买。(另一方面，迈克有几个域名的购买量超过了这个数字)。我个人对花这么多钱买域名几乎没有什么担心或怀疑，因为它几乎完全没有风险：域名总是有一些内在的价值在里面。不过，正如我将在本系列的下一部分写到的那样，我们最近承担了一些支出，这些支出几乎同样大，但却不那么无风险。
